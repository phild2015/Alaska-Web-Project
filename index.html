Haiku 

  • waking far from home  
    the sunrise songs  
    of unknown birds  
     ⚘ Charles B. Dickson 
  • soft rain — 
    a baby moose nudges  
    it's mothers flank  
     ⚘ Cindy Zachowitz 
  • riverbank mud –– 
    so still, the water  
    in my footprints  
     ⚘ Mark Arvid White 
  • majestic mountain  
    steadfast against life's tempests
    strengthens the spirit  
     ⚘ Mihaela Pirjol 
  • tea colored creek  
    live salmon make their way  
    past the dead  
     ⚘ Cindy Zachowitz 
  • journey's end  
    far off in the blue distance  
    dark clouds  
     ⚘ Billie Wilson 
  • pale pink band stretches  
    across the sky—pops the spruce  
    into sharp relief  
     ⚘ Jenna Schnuer 
  • in the drydock sling 
    the boat lowers to the sea — 
    a high call of gulls 
     ⚘ Billie Wilson 

➹Day 1

June 4th, 2011

After night at campsite at park entrance packed up, checked gear and went through briefing at rangers cabin, watched bear videos, hilarious…received bear-proof food and poop (wtf?!) container. Left itinerary and whereabouts with ranger log. Took a short hike to get warmed up and ran into moose (pic checkpoint and moose) marmot and chipmunk and porcupine. Goosebumps and butterflies for next day - on my own out in the wilderness. (pic glacial silt, scenery)

➹Day 2

June 5th, 2011

Hop on bus out to wilderness. Get dropped off with wolves. Make a prayer before I head out into the my glacial valley. Start trek down icy creek to XXX river say hi to porcupine. Find a wide braided part to cross river and follow valley up towards mountains in the distance. An eagle follows me for a good portion of the treck (maybe I'm on the menu?). After 6 hour hike find a promontory with a creek and beautiful view of the valley to set up camp. Fall asleep quickly even though it's bright.
